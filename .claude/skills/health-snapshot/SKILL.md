## Health Snapshot

**Key Metrics to Track**

| Metric | Healthy Range | How to Measure |
|---|---|---|
| Resting Heart Rate | 60–100 bpm | Morning, before getting up |
| Blood Pressure | <120/80 mmHg | Home cuff or pharmacy |
| BMI | 18.5–24.9 | Weight (kg) ÷ Height (m²) |
| Waist Circumference | <35" (F) / <40" (M) | At navel, relaxed |
| Resting O2 Saturation | 95–100% | Pulse oximeter |

**Fitness Self-Assessment**

Provide a simple 3-level self-assessment (beginner / intermediate / advanced) based on:
- Cardiovascular: how many minutes can they sustain moderate cardio without stopping?
- Strength: can they do 10 push-ups / 20 bodyweight squats with good form?
- Flexibility: can they touch their toes standing?

**Baseline Habits Checklist**
- Current average daily steps
- Hours of sleep per night
- Water intake per day
- Frequency of exercise per week
- Stress level (self-rated 1–10)

**When to See a Doctor**
List 5–6 specific signs or situations where a physician consult is recommended before starting a new health program (e.g., chest pain, family history, BMI >30, uncontrolled blood pressure).

**Tracking Tools**
Recommend 3 free or low-cost apps/devices for tracking the above metrics, with one sentence on what each does best.
