**Account Types Explained**

| Account | Tax Treatment | 2025 Limit | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| 401k (Traditional) | Pre-tax contributions, taxed on withdrawal | $23,500 | High earners now, lower income in retirement |
| Roth 401k | After-tax contributions, tax-free growth | $23,500 | Younger investors, expect higher income later |
| Traditional IRA | Pre-tax (if eligible), taxed on withdrawal | $7,000 | Additional retirement savings |
| Roth IRA | After-tax, tax-free growth + withdrawals | $7,000 | Income <$161k single / <$240k married |
| HSA | Triple tax advantage | $4,300 single | High-deductible health plan holders |
| Brokerage | After-tax, capital gains taxed | None | Goals beyond retirement accounts |

**Common Beginner Mistakes**
1. Waiting until you have "enough" to start
2. Panic-selling during market downturns
3. Picking individual stocks without understanding the company
4. Ignoring fees — even 1% expense ratio compounds painfully
5. Not increasing contributions when income rises
6. Treating a Roth IRA like a savings account (not investing after contributing)
